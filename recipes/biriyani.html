Biriyani

Everyone knows Biriyani!!! I am sure you will love it!

Biryani is a mixed rice dish originating among the Muslims of South Asia. It is made with Indian spices, vegetables, rice, and usually some type of meat, or in some cases without any meat, and sometimes, in addition, eggs and potatoes.

Ingredients

  • Mutton, cut into pieces
  • Basmati rice
  • Onions, thinly sliced
  • Tomatoes, chopped
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • Yogurt
  • Biryani masala powder
  • Turmeric powder
  • Red chili powder
  • Garam masala powder
  • Cumin seeds
  • Cinnamon stick
  • Green cardamom pods
  • Cloves
  • Bay leaves
  • Saffron strands (optional)
  • Milk (optional, for saffron milk)
  • Ghee (clarified butter)
  • Vegetable oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves (cilantro), chopped
  • Fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • Lemon, sliced
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Rinse the basmati rice thoroughly in cold water and soak it in water for about 30 minutes. Drain the rice and set it aside.
  2. Heat ghee and vegetable oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pan or a pressure cooker.
  3. Add cumin seeds, cinnamon stick, green cardamom pods, cloves, and bay leaves to the hot oil. Sauté them until they release their aroma.
  4. Add thinly sliced onions to the pan and cook them until they turn golden brown. Keep stirring occasionally to ensure even browning.
  5. Now, add ginger-garlic paste to the pan and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  6. Add the mutton pieces to the pan and cook them on medium heat until they are browned on all sides.
  7. Add chopped tomatoes, green chilies, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and biryani masala powder to the pan. Mix well and cook for a few minutes until the tomatoes soften.
  8. Add yogurt to the mixture and stir it in. Let it simmer for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  9. Meanwhile, in a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Add salt and the soaked, drained basmati rice to the boiling water. Cook the rice until it is 70-80% cooked (parboiled). Drain the rice and set it aside.
  10. Once the mutton is cooked and tender, remove half of the mutton mixture from the pan and set it aside.
  11. Spread the remaining mutton mixture evenly in the pan. Layer the parboiled rice over the mutton layer. Make sure to spread it evenly.
  12. Optional: Dissolve a pinch of saffron strands in warm milk and drizzle it over the rice for added flavor and color.
  13. Sprinkle the chopped fresh mint leaves and coriander leaves over the rice. Place the sliced lemons on top.
  14. Cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid. Cook the biryani on low heat for about 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the rice to fully cook through.
  15. Once done, turn off the heat and let the biryani rest for a few minutes, allowing the steam to settle.
  16. Gently fluff the biryani with a fork, mixing the layers of rice and mutton.
  17. Serve the aromatic mutton biryani hot with raita (yogurt sauce) or salan (spicy curry) on the side
