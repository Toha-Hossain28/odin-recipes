Egg Pudding

Fullfill your desire with a dessert

puddings are nearly always sweet desserts of milk or fruit juice variously flavoured and thickened with cornstarch, arrowroot, flour, tapioca, rice, bread, or eggs.

Ingredients

  • Eggs
  • Milk
  • Sugar
  • Vanilla extract

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C) and prepare a baking dish or individual ramekins by greasing them lightly with butter or cooking spray.
  2. In a mixing bowl, crack the eggs and beat them until they are well combined.
  3. Add sugar to the beaten eggs and whisk again until the sugar is dissolved and the mixture becomes smooth.
  4. Pour in the milk and vanilla extract. Whisk everything together until all the ingredients are thoroughly combined.
  5. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into another bowl or directly into a pouring jug. This step helps remove any lumps and ensures a smoother pudding texture.
  6. Pour the strained mixture into the greased baking dish or ramekins. If using a single baking dish, make sure it is large enough to hold the entire mixture. If using ramekins, distribute the mixture evenly among them.
  7. Optional: Sprinkle a pinch of cinnamon powder or nutmeg on top of the pudding mixture for added flavor.
  8. Place the baking dish or ramekins in a larger baking pan or tray. Carefully pour hot water into the larger pan, creating a water bath (also known as a bain-marie) around the pudding dish(es). The water level should reach about halfway up the sides of the dish(es).
  9. Carefully transfer the baking pan with the pudding mixture and water bath into the preheated oven.
  10. Bake the pudding for about 45-50 minutes or until it is set. You can check the doneness by inserting a toothpick or knife into the center of the pudding; if it comes out clean, the pudding is ready.
  11. Once baked, remove the pudding from the oven and let it cool to room temperature. Then, transfer it to the refrigerator and chill for at least a couple of hours or until it is fully set and firm.
  12. Serve the egg pudding chilled, either directly from the baking dish or by gently inverting it onto a serving plate. You can garnish it with a sprinkle of cinnamon powder or additional toppings like whipped cream, fresh fruits, or chopped nuts, if desired.
